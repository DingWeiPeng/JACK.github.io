FormerCodec Demo

Abstract

Over the past few years, the bandwidth bottleneck in satellite Internet of Things (IoT) has driven the development of new methods to compress transmitted speech. For satellite voice communications, it is crucial to achieve high-quality codecs with a code rate below 1 kbps, compatible with channels like Beidou-3 that often operate at less than 1 kbps. Neural network vocoders have emerged as a promising approach in the AI community, aiming to provide high-fidelity audio compression. In this paper, we propose FormerCodec, a causal speech codec capable of operating across various bit rates while delivering high-quality audio at low bit rates for satellite voice transmission. FormerCodec employs a Transformer language model to predict tokens, reducing the bit rate by 25\% while maintaining decoded audio quality. Our experimental results demonstrate that FormerCodec provides high-quality decoded audio at 0.76 kbps and 1.15 kbps. Notably, the MUSHRA (MUlti Stimulus test with Hidden Reference and Anchor) score of FormerCodec at 0.76 kbps surpasses that of Encodec at 1.5 kbps. Demo audio, including self-recorded speeches, is available at https://dingweipeng.github.io/JACK.github.io. FormerCodec offers a novel approach and solution for satellite voice communication with limited bandwidth.

Tips

We demonstrate decoded speech with Transformer predictor for its effectiveness in improving decoded speech quality at ultra-low bitrate less than 1kbps.
